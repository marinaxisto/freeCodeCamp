Classic Brazilian Brigadeiro

This recipe will show you how to make Brazil's most famous and beloved chocolate truffle. It's a simple, no-bake treat perfect for any occasion! üç´

Ingredients

  • 1 can (14 oz) sweetened condensed milk
  • 1/4 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1 cup chocolate sprinkles, for rolling

Instructions

  1. In a medium saucepan, combine the condensed milk, cocoa powder, and butter.
  2. Cook over medium-low heat, stirring constantly with a wooden spoon or spatula until the mixture thickens and starts pulling away from the bottom and sides of the pan (about 10-15 minutes).
  3. Pour the mixture onto a greased plate and let it cool to room temperature.
  4. Once cooled, grease your hands with a little butter, take a small amount of the mixture, and roll it into a 1-inch ball.
  5. Roll the ball in the chocolate sprinkles until it's fully covered, and place it in a small paper or foil candy cup. Repeat with the rest of the mixture.
