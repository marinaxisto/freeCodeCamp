A Journey Through Brazilian Music

Brazil has a rich and diverse musical history that has influenced artists worldwide. This page explores three of its most iconic genres: Samba, Bossa Nova, and MPB.

Samba

Samba is a Brazilian musical genre and dance style, with its roots in Africa via the West African slave trade and religious traditions. It is recognized around the world as a symbol of Brazil and the Brazilian Carnival.

Bossa Nova

Bossa Nova, which translates to "new trend" or "new wave," emerged in the 1950s and 1960s. It's a lyrical fusion of samba and jazz, known for its smooth, melodic style, as famously demonstrated in "The Girl from Ipanema."

MPB (Música Popular Brasileira)

Música Popular Brasileira (or MPB) is a trend in post-bossa nova urban popular music in Brazil that revisits typical Brazilian styles. It blends various influences, from samba and bossa nova to international pop, rock, and jazz.
